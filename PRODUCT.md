# Product

## Register

product

## Users

SorsorRank is for young, online Thai users and civic-curious friend groups who want a quick daily participation ritual without being pushed toward a political conclusion. Secondary users include researchers, journalists, and operators who may inspect aggregate curiosity trends after enough participation exists.

Users are usually on mobile, often coming from a shared link in a chat or social feed. They need to understand the rules quickly, consent before stored interaction data is collected, complete 10 sparse cards, and optionally view aggregate Research Interest Rank.

## Product Purpose

SorsorRank makes civic research feel like a light daily challenge. The product shows public-figure names, sends users outward to Google Search, records only consented Research or Skip actions, and publishes aggregate research-interest rankings after safety thresholds are met.

Success means people finish the daily 10, share neutral completion cards, bring friends into the same consented loop, and understand that the output is aggregate curiosity, not a poll, endorsement, prediction, approval signal, or voting guide.

## Brand Personality

Fast, calm, tactile.

The interface should feel like a clean civic game: energetic enough to complete in a group chat, restrained enough for political context, and sparse enough that the app never looks like it is making political claims.

## Anti-references

Do not make SorsorRank look or behave like a dating app, candidate campaign page, debate feed, prediction market, political identity quiz, gossip board, or approval-rating dashboard.

Avoid hearts, flame icons, red and green moral judgment colors, romantic metaphors, head-to-head battles, politician-specific share cards, comments, quote posts, AI summaries, search snippets, allegations, accusations, and hosted political biographies.

## Design Principles

1. Participation is the fun part. Design share moments around finishing the daily 10, not around preferences for public figures.
2. Cards stay sparse. A card can show name, optional role or party, a Google Search handoff, and Research or Skip actions. It should not host claims.
3. Consent is part of the product, not a compliance screen bolted on later. Swipes must not be stored before explicit consent.
4. Rankings are aggregate and thresholded. Visual language must avoid popularity, victory, approval, support, and prediction cues.
5. Mobile speed matters. The app should feel instant on mobile data, with lightweight Preact, plain CSS, and transform-based motion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for color contrast, focus visibility, touch targets, and keyboard fallback. Support both Thai and English names, including long names, mixed scripts, and narrow mobile screens.

Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Use clear text labels alongside icons. Every public-facing state should have loading, empty, error, success, offline or slow-network, long-name, and small-mobile behavior.
