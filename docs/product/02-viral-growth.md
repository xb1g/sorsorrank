# Viral Growth Plan

## Growth Thesis

The app spreads when people share that they swiped, not who they support.

Bad civic virality turns users into campaign amplifiers. Good SorsorRank virality turns users into hosts of a daily swipe challenge — the political Tinder your group chat didn't know it needed.

## Core Loop

```
User opens app
  -> completes Daily Deck (10 swipes)
  -> sees Match Card with flame/heart moment
  -> shares Match Card to group chat
  -> friend taps link
  -> friend completes their Daily Deck
  -> Crush Rank becomes more interesting
  -> friend checks Crush Rank
  -> friend sends it to their group chat
```

## Viral Surfaces

### 1. Match Card

Primary share card after the 10th swipe.

Copy options:

- "I matched with 10 public figures today. Your turn."
- "Swiped my Daily Deck on SorsorRank."
- "10 swipes. Zero homework. Find your political crush."
- "Crush Rank updated. Did you swipe today?"

Rules:

- No politician names by default.
- No crush/pass disclosure.
- Include app name, date, challenge link, and a link to today's Crush Rank.
- Hearts and flame icons allowed.

### 2. Hot Streak Card

Unlocked after repeated completions.

Copy options:

- "🔥 3-day Hot Streak on SorsorRank."
- "I haven't missed my Daily Deck in 5 days."
- "Daily Deck streak: 7 days and counting."

Rules:

- Streak is about habit, not ideology.
- Do not show political preference history.
- Fire icon encouraged.

### 3. Crush Rank Snapshot

Shareable public Crush Rank preview.

Copy options:

- "Today's Crush Rank — who is Thailand curious about?"
- "Collective crush energy, not a poll."
- "Top crushes this week."

Required elements:

- Date range.
- Sample size.
- Methodology link ("How Crush Rank works").
- Disclaimer: "Not a poll or endorsement. Crush = research curiosity."

### 4. Friend Challenge

Light prompt after completion:

- "Send this to one friend who follows public affairs."
- "Make your group chat swipe their 10."
- "Compare collective crush energy, not conclusions."
- "Battle Mode: challenge a friend to see who your group crushes on most."

Rules:

- Challenge friends to participate, not to support a person.
- No referral rewards tied to politicians.

### 5. Match % Reveal

After completing Daily Deck, user can see each figure's Match %:

- "82% of today's swipers crushed on this figure."
- Shown only above sample threshold.
- Label: "Not a poll — crush = research curiosity."

This creates shareable moments: "Wait, only 12% crushed on this person?!"

## Fun Mechanics

- 10-card Daily Deck creates daily scarcity.
- Tinder-style swipe physics: drag, tilt, snap-away.
- Progress meter: `7/10`.
- Match Card confetti moment after card 10.
- Hot Streak count with fire icon.
- "Come back tomorrow" lockout state.
- Mystery reveal: Crush Rank unlocks after swiping, with read-only methodology path for users who decline consent.
- Battle Mode: two-figure showdown for secondary engagement (outside election windows).

## Viral Safety Boundaries

Do not build:

- "I crushed X" or "I passed X" share cards that name figures.
- Public political identity badges.
- Comment wars.
- Quote posts attached to politicians.
- Paid boosts.
- Paid or unpaid influencer seeding tied to an active election.
- Targeted ads based on location, inferred politics, demographics, or public-figure interest.
- Candidate, party, campaign, PAC, or proxy coordination.
- Campaign pages.
- AI-generated roasts, praise, summaries, allegations, or memes.
- Battle Mode during any covered election window or for any active-candidate roster row.
- Romantic or sexual framing in any share surface.

## Launch Playbook

### Private Alpha

- Roster: 20-50 well-known public figures.
- Invite: friends only.
- Goal: test completion, share, and confusion rate.
- Watch: whether people misread Crush Rank as romantic preference or voting support.
- Public Crush Rank: off.
- Battle Mode: off.
- Active candidates: excluded unless counsel explicitly approves.

### Public Soft Launch

- Requires Thai counsel approval of exact copy, flow, roster, privacy notice, methodology, vendors, and timing.
- Do not launch during a covered election window.
- Add "How Crush Rank Works" methodology page before public Crush Rank.
- Enable Match Card share.
- Keep Crush Rank labels conservative; include "not a poll" disclaimer.
- Add takedown/contact link in footer.
- Monitor copy screenshots people share on social media.
- Stop public Crush Rank if screenshots or user behavior frame the product as support, opposition, voting advice, or campaign momentum.

### Viral Push

- Only after soft-launch safety metrics are acceptable and no active election freeze applies.
- Launch with the "Swipe your 10" challenge.
- Seed group chats and friend circles.
- Share only Match Cards and Crush Rank snapshots.
- Avoid figure-specific promotion.
- Enable Battle Mode if outside election window and safety review passes.
- Do not buy traffic, boost posts, or compensate creators during election-sensitive periods.

## North Star

Daily completed Daily Deck sessions and viral Match Card shares.

The product wins if users come back and make friends swipe their 10. It loses if users treat it as an election prediction, a romantic matchmaker, or a weapon for attacking public figures.
