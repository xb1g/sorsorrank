# Viral Growth Plan

## Growth Thesis

The app spreads when people share that they participated, not who they support.

Bad civic virality turns users into campaign amplifiers. Good SorsorRank virality turns users into hosts of a daily research challenge.

## Core Loop

```
User opens app
  -> completes 10 swipes
  -> sees satisfying completion moment
  -> shares neutral card
  -> friend taps link
  -> friend completes 10 swipes
  -> aggregate rank becomes more interesting
```

## Viral Surfaces

### 1. Completion Card

Primary share card after the 10th swipe.

Copy options:

- "I researched 10 public figures today. Your turn."
- "I did my 10 on SorsorRank."
- "10 swipes. Zero homework. Go research yours."
- "Today's civic curiosity check: done."

Rules:

- No politician names by default.
- No support/skip disclosure.
- Include app name, date, and challenge link.

### 2. Streak Card

Unlocked after repeated completions.

Copy options:

- "3-day research streak."
- "I kept up with my 10."
- "Civic curiosity streak: 5 days."

Rules:

- Streak is about habit, not ideology.
- Do not show political preference history.

### 3. Rank Snapshot

Shareable public ranking preview.

Copy options:

- "Today's Research Interest Rank"
- "Who are people researching today?"
- "Aggregate curiosity, not a poll."

Required elements:

- Date range.
- Sample size.
- Methodology link.
- Disclaimer: "Not a poll or endorsement."

### 4. Friend Challenge

Light prompt after completion:

- "Send this to one friend who follows public affairs."
- "Make your group chat do the 10."
- "Compare curiosity, not conclusions."

Rules:

- Challenge friends to participate, not to support a person.
- No referral rewards tied to politicians.

## Fun Mechanics

- 10-card daily limit creates scarcity.
- Card stack animation makes it tactile.
- Progress meter: `7/10`.
- End screen confetti-style moment, but visually restrained.
- Streak count for repeated completions.
- "Come back tomorrow" lockout state.
- Mystery reveal: rankings unlock after doing the 10, with a read-only methodology path for users who decline consent.

## Viral Safety Boundaries

Do not build:

- "I support X" share cards.
- "I skipped X" share cards.
- Head-to-head politician battles.
- Public political identity badges.
- Comment wars.
- Quote posts attached to politicians.
- Paid boosts.
- Paid or unpaid influencer seeding tied to an active election.
- Targeted ads based on location, inferred politics, demographics, or public-figure interest.
- Candidate, party, campaign, PAC, or proxy coordination.
- Campaign pages.
- AI-generated roasts, praise, summaries, allegations, or memes.

## Launch Playbook

### Private Alpha

- Roster: 20-50 well-known public figures.
- Invite: friends only.
- Goal: test completion, share, and confusion rate.
- Watch: whether people misread the rank as voting support.
- Public ranks: off.
- Active candidates: excluded unless counsel explicitly approves.

### Public Soft Launch

- Requires Thai counsel approval of exact copy, flow, roster, privacy notice, methodology, vendors, and timing.
- Do not launch during a covered election window.
- Add methodology page before public ranking.
- Enable share cards.
- Keep rank labels conservative.
- Add takedown/contact link in footer.
- Monitor copy screenshots people share on social media.
- Stop public ranking if screenshots or user behavior frame the product as support, opposition, voting advice, or campaign momentum.

### Viral Push

- Only after soft-launch safety metrics are acceptable and no active election freeze applies.
- Launch with the "Do your 10" challenge.
- Seed group chats and friend circles.
- Share only completion cards and rank snapshots.
- Avoid candidate-specific promotion.
- Do not buy traffic, boost posts, or compensate creators during election-sensitive periods.

## North Star

Daily completed research sessions.

The product wins if users come back and make friends do their 10. It loses if users treat it as an election prediction or a weapon for attacking public figures.
